The Reality on Our Roads

Hard work, no record to show for it.

Across East Africa, a million riders move the economy on two wheels — yet they remain unprotected, unbanked, and unseen. LUNO was built to change that.

Notebook chaos

Payments tracked in tattered notebooks and memory. Lost receipts and disputed totals strain the trust between owner and driver.

No financial identity

Without records, hard-working drivers can't access loans, insurance, or recognition — trapped outside the formal economy.

No safety net

When a driver is attacked on the road, help is far away and families learn too late. Dignity and safety should not be a luxury.

How It Works

From first ride to full ownership.

Four simple steps turn a rent-to-own arrangement into a clear, dignified journey — for both driver and owner.

1

Set the contract

The owner registers the bike, the driver, and a weekly payment. LUNO calculates the path to ownership.

2

Pay & verify

The driver pays via M-Pesa or cash. Each payment is recorded and verified — permanent digital proof.

3

Watch it grow

The ownership ring fills in real time. The driver sees their exact freedom date draw closer every week.

4

Own it

The final payment lands. The bike is theirs — and a verified financial identity is born for life.

Everything you might ask.

Yes. Drivers never pay to use LUNO. The owner covers a small platform fee — the driver gets safety, proof of every payment, and a clear path to owning their bike, at no cost.

LUNO is offline-first. Payments and records are saved on the phone and sync automatically once a connection returns — built for real conditions on low-end Android devices.

A hold-to-fire button sends the driver’s live GPS location to their owner and next-of-kin instantly. It is a lifeline for the driver — never a remote kill-switch on the vehicle.

Your data is handled under Tanzania’s data-protection law, with consent, and is used to benefit you — for example, to help you qualify for loans or insurance. It is never sold against your interest.

LUNO is live with drivers in Dar es Salaam, Arusha, and Mwanza, with expansion across Tanzania and East Africa underway.
